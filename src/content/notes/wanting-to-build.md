title: "Wanting to build something, not just code it"
date: 2026-07-19
kind: changed-my-mind

---

For a while I assumed the natural next step from data science was just more of
the same — better models, bigger pipelines, more code. I've started to think
differently, and it's why I'm reaching toward robotics.

To be clear about what this isn't: my focus hasn't shifted. ML, modelling, and
the LLM / RAG work are still the centre of what I do, and I'm not walking away
from any of it. This isn't a pivot.

What changed is a want I'd been ignoring. Almost everything I've built so far
lives on a screen — it runs, it answers, and then I close the laptop. I want
to build something that actually exists in the physical world: something that
has to deal with wind and noise and a battery running down, not just an input
and an output. I've spent enough time only writing code; I want some of it to
move something.

Robotics is where those two things meet. It's still modelling and inference
and being honest about uncertainty, but pointed at a machine that has to act
in the real world instead of a plot on a screen. That's the part I want to sit
with next.
