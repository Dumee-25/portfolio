Been sketching out an idea after a discussion with my supervisor: a
search-and-rescue drone that fuses a few different sensors — camera, Wi-Fi,
microphone — into one map of where a missing person might be, and stays
honest about how confident that map actually is as sensors drop out or the
radio link degrades.

I wrote up a report on it myself, on my own time, mostly to work out whether
the idea holds together. It hasn't been proposed or approved as an actual
project yet, and the design will likely change a fair amount once it's been
properly reviewed.

Worth being upfront about where I'm starting from: this is my first robotics
project, and I only started learning ROS 2 at the end of May. Most of the
more interesting design decisions here came out of conversation, not from
things I already knew going in.

More on this once there's something more settled to report.
